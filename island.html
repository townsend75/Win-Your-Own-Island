Filicudi- An Italian Island Paradise

Filicudi, one of seven volcanic islands making up the Aeolian Archipelago, lies just 35 miles northeast of Sicily, Southern Italy. Said to be named after Aeolus, the mythical ruler of the winds, 75% of hte island is now a natural reserve, growing wine, olive oil, grain and vegetables

Cosa Nostra?

In 1971, Filicudi was used by Italian police to exile 18 allegad Mafia bosses. Within five days, the entire population of 270 had left in protest. Now, around 200 people call the island home- and we think you should let them stay if you win!!
